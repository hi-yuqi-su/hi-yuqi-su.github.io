We designed and implemented an in-cabin distributed acoustic sensing system for child-presence detection. We build a multi-speaker signal-superposition model, design a multi-microphone multiplication technique to amplify tiny body movements, and develop a second-order reflection multipath-combination enhancement algorithm. The system can extract subtle respiration even under occlusion, enabling low-cost, full-coverage child-presence detection.

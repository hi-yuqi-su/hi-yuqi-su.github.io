We designed and implemented an acoustic-sensing-based automotive digital-key system. To overcome the phase-ambiguity problem of sparse arrays, we propose a multi-frequency virtual-microphone technique and co-prime multi-frequency waveforms. With only **3 physical microphones**, the system achieves accuracy equivalent to a **21-microphone array** and remains stable in real-world interference scenarios such as parking lots, offering a cost-effective localization solution for digital car keys.
